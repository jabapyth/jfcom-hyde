Vanilla Android Music Player

Posted: Sat, 11 Aug 2012
  • android
  • vanilla
  • app
  • open-source

So I just got myself an LG-G2x android smartphone [running eaglesblood ICS :)] and I’ve been trying a lot of things out. One thing that I really wanted to get right was a music player app. I looked around at the many reviews of the (mostly adware or freemium) popular music apps, thinking to myself “there’s gotta be a really good, free, even open-source music player. somewhere”. well, I stumbled across “vanilla” early on, but was scared away by the somewhat primitive and downright clucky looking graphics [see the screenshots on google play or AndroidOpenSourceApplications ]. But, after more searching and finding nothing promising, I decided to give it a spin.

So I grabbed the source from github, compiled it and gave it a ride on my g2x, and…it’s beautiful! At least, much more than the aformentioned screenshots would attest. It looks really sharp, actually blending in perfectly with the UI, and packing some surprise goodies like an integrated file-browser and lock screen support. Granted, the lock screen could use some tweaking, but that’s what fork and pull-request are for!

Anyway, to save the next guy some trouble, I’ve taken some screenshots of this app in action. (running at commit 496d28411a )
